Events


DAY 1

May 24

Santa Cruz Beach Boardwalk

10 am - 5 pm

The festival's opening event will be a beach cleanup and coastal restoration project held on May 24th. Participants will gather at the Santa Cruz Beach Boardwalk, equipped with gloves and trash bags, to remove litter and debris, followed by a discussion led by Sea Shepherd volunteers on the importance of preserving marine habitats.


DAY 2

May 25

Santa Cruz Beach Boardwalk

10 am - 8 pm

Morning: Guided kayak tours of Santa Cruz’s West Cliff area, allowing participants to observe marine wildlife in their natural habitat while learning about conservation efforts to protect these ecosystems.

Afternoon: Film screenings showcasing documentaries on marine wildlife conservation and Sea Shepherd's campaigns. Q&A sessions with filmmakers and Sea Shepherd representatives will follow each screening.


DAY 3

May 26

Santa Cruz Beach Boardwalk

10 am - 11 pm

Morning: Educational workshops and panel discussions featuring marine biologists, conservationists, and Sea Shepherd crew members. Topics will include marine biodiversity, the impact of climate change on ocean ecosystems, and strategies for sustainable fishing practices.

Afternoon: Live music performances by Green Day, Radiohead, and The Roots (artists that all support ocean conservation) creating a festive atmosphere for attendees to unwind and celebrate the success of the festival.
